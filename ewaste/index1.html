About E-waste


E-waste contains elements that are harmful and deadly. E-waste contains materials known to pollute and harm the environment, and is a large factor in greenhouse gasses, contributing to climate change.

E-waste: Discarded electronic devices which are often obsolete that contain harmful materials.

Apple has sold over a billion iPhones as of July 27, 2016. Most of these are already obsolete, and end up landfills, along with old computer monitors, hard drives, printers, televisions, VCR's and countless other obsolescent technologies. https://www.epa.gov/recycle/electronics-donation-and-recycling

Facts about E-waste from DoSomething.org

1. In 2009, discarded TVs, computers, peripherals (including printers, scanners, fax machines) mice, keyboards, and cell phones totaled about 2.37 million short tons.

2. E-waste represents 2% of America's trash in landfills, but it equals 70% of overall toxic waste.

3. 20 to 50 million metric tons of e-waste are disposed worldwide every year.

4. Cell phones and other electronic items contain high amounts of precious metals like gold or silver. Americans dump phones containing over $60 million in gold/silver every year.

5. A large number of what is labeled as "e-waste" is actually not waste at all, but rather whole electronic equipment or parts that are readily marketable for reuse or can be recycled for materials recovery.

6. Only 12.5% of e-waste is currently recycled.

7. For every 1 million cell phones that are recycled, 35,274 lbs of copper, 772 lbs of silver, 75 lbs of gold, and 33 lbs of palladium can be recovered.

8. Recycling 1 million laptops saves the energy equivalent to the electricity used by 3,657 U.S. homes in a year.

9. E-waste is still the fastest growing municipal waste stream in America, according to the EPA.

10. It takes 530 lbs of fossil fuel, 48 lbs of chemicals, and 1.5 tons of water to manufacture one computer and monitor.

Electronic items that are considered to be hazardous include, but are not limited to: Televisions and computer monitors that contain cathode ray tubes, LCD desktop monitors, LCD televisions, Plasma televisions, Portable DVD players with LCD screens.

Values


MISSION: Our mission is to help significantly reduce e-waste by engaging and inspiring people to create a meaningful alternative for their e-waste.


VISION: Our vision is one that brings a host of communitites together for the purposes of reducing the same e-waste that is so easily transformed into something of permanent value. By inspiring creativity, a passionte newly formed group of persons concerned and excited about saving the environment has formed, ready to support and teach, but most importantly: make some really cool stuff!
